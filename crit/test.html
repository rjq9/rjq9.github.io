Our Mission

The Dal2n School is committed to providing each and every student with the abilities and motivation needed to excel in the fields of their own interest. Through a rigorous education - of each student's making - the child at Dalton emerges highly skilled in the subjects they love, and cultivates the agency and intellectual curiosity needed to push their field and the world forward.

Dal2n is committed to the following principles:

  • Cultivating the highest levels of intellectual success.
    Our goal - Ill just say it - was to finally get Fieldston to stop with all the bragging about the whole Oppenheimer thing. Now, old Oppie never won a Nobel, so we thought our goal would be: optimize Dalt2n students to win a Nobel prize. (No Fieldston grad has ever won a Nobel. Although one of them did start Zabars, which honestly may be a more enduring legacy.) Beyond that original plan, we wanted to ask: how could we optimize Dalton for pure intellectual output? (To put it another way: how could we make Dalton like a magnet school?) What would we change? Of course, we ought to widen our horizons a little. Plenty of fields dont have Nobels. So we ought to generalize, which left us with the primary goal you see here: reach heights in intellectual pursuits. This can mean a lot! Maybe some student wins a Pulitzer. Or wins enduring fame in the art of underwater basket weaving.
  • Developing intellectual independence and risk-taking through inquiry, direct experience, and collaboration.
    Here, weve taken directly from the Dalton mission statement as it stands. Given that the whole premise of Dalt2n is that students spend all their time on the thing they both enjoy and are good at, it wouldnt really make sense to put them in a system where they have no control over their own education. Additionally, research in the real world is purely collaborative, and must stem from genuine curiosity to be successful.
  • Promoting the use of intellectual pursuits for the common good of the world.
    As it turns out, its hard to win a Nobel if students only use their intellectual ability to make money off people who dont know what theyre doing. The winners have done something that actually benefits the world. Fortunately, Profit isnt one of our major tracks. Dalt2n focuses on studies that will impact the world (or at least a part of it) for the years to come.

Our Values
Cultivating Character, Creating Community

Central to Dalton’s commitment to its students is providing an education of excellence where students are empowered and character is cultivated in an inclusive, intentionally diverse school community. Helen Parkhurst’s vision, rooted in the Dalton Plan, provides a uniquely supportive foundation upon which to build strong community values. Community building and Dalton’s eight core values are woven into the fabric of the curricula throughout all three divisions.

List of 8 items.

  • Valuing truth, learning, and authenticity

  • Practicing and promoting respect for self, for others, and for the environment we share

  • Acting with empathy and compassion

  • Developing the character and confidence required to take risks in civic and intellectual undertakings

  • Practicing and promoting integrity and responsibility

  • Encouraging leadership

  • Making responsible choices in a variety of settings and participating constructively in the community beyond the school

  • Insisting upon justice
